Delta

The Delta models and impulse or point mass. The continuous Dirac delta is defined by two properties:

The first property states that the delta is zero everywhere but the origin (or the center of symmetry). This implies that the Dirac delta has infinitesimal support.

The second property states that the Dirac delta has a finite area.

In the discrete case, a different definition of the delta, called the Kronecker delta, is used. This delta is zero everywhere but the center of symmetry, where it has an amplitude of 1.

Parameters:

  • Amplitude, A0
  • Center, x0

Support: infinitesimal (Dirac delta) or a single discrete index (Kronecker delta)

Area: 1 (Dirac delta)

Symmetry: even
