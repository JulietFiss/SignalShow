Rayleigh Noise

The Rayleigh noise may be generated by selecting two independent random variables from identical Gaussian distributions. Using one of the independent variables as the x-component of a vector and the other as the y-component of the vector, the magnitude of the vector is calculated to produce a new random variable. The probability density function of a Rayleigh distribution is characterized by a single parameter, a

Parameters:

  • a
  • seed, the seed used for the pseudorandom number generator.
