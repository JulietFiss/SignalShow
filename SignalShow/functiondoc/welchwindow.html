Welch Window

The Welch apodization window is a downward-sloping parabola that is zero outside of its x-intercepts.

Apodization windows are used when taking the FFT of discrete data. The FFT assumes that the N-sample array is periodic. If this is not the case, the resulting transform will exhibit "false" frequency content known as "leakage" due to the discontinuity at the edges of the array. Apodization windows have zero amplitude (or nearly so) at the array "edges", and may be used to modulate nonperiodic input to ensure that the product function is periodic over N samples.

Parameters:

  • Amplitude, A0
  • Center, x0
  • Width, b
