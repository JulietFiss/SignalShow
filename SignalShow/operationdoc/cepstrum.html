Cepstrum

The complex cepstrum was defined by Oppenheim in his development of homomorphic system theory. It may be defined * verbally: the complex cepstrum (of a signal) is the Fourier transform of the logarithm (with unwrapped phase) of the Fourier transform (of a signal). Sometimes called the spectrum of a spectrum. * mathematically: complex cepstrum of signal = FT(log(|FT(the signal)|)+j2¹m) (where m is the integer required to properly unwrap the angle or imaginary part of the complex log function) * algorithmically: signal -> FT -> abs() -> log -> phase unwrapping -> FT -> cepstrum
